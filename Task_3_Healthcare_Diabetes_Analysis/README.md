# 🩺 Advanced Healthcare Analytics: Diabetes Risk Factor Analysis

> **ShadowFox Internship — Task 3 (Advanced Level)**  
> A comprehensive healthcare analytics project focused on identifying major diabetes risk factors using exploratory data analysis, statistical techniques, and advanced healthcare visualizations.

---

## 📌 Project Overview

Diabetes is one of the most significant chronic health conditions affecting millions of individuals worldwide. Early identification of diabetes risk factors plays an important role in preventive healthcare and patient management.

This project presents an **advanced healthcare analytics study** using the **Pima Indians Diabetes Dataset**, with the objective of identifying the healthcare indicators most strongly associated with diabetes occurrence.

The analysis includes:

- Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
- Data Cleaning & Preprocessing
- Feature Engineering
- Statistical Analysis
- Healthcare Risk Profiling
- Correlation Analysis
- Advanced Visualizations
- Preventive Healthcare Recommendations

---

## 🎯 Project Objectives

The primary objectives of this study are:

- Explore and understand healthcare patient data
- Identify major diabetes risk indicators
- Analyze relationships among healthcare variables

- Support preventive healthcare recommendations

## 🔍 Key Findings

### 🩸 Glucose Level
Glucose emerged as one of the strongest healthcare indicators associated with diabetes occurrence.

### ⚖️ BMI Association
Overweight and obese patients demonstrated greater diabetes prevalence.

### 👴 Age Influence
Older patient groups showed comparatively higher diabetes occurrence.

### 🔗 Healthcare Variable Relationships
Correlation analysis revealed meaningful relationships among glucose, BMI, insulin, and diabetes outcome.

### 🏥 High-Risk Patient Groups
Patients with elevated glucose levels, higher BMI, and older age showed increased diabetes vulnerability.
